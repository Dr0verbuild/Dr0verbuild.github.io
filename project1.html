Introduction

 Save the World is a game about defending objectives from zombie-like “husks,” using buildings, traps, and weapons. However, Save the World is a complicated game with a confusing tutorial, and boring onboarding. With that in mind, providing instructions can help explain how to understand the game enough to even have the potential to enjoy playing it. These instructions will begin by explaining core mechanics and then go deeper into the steps needed to create a loadout that achieves the goal that the player is seeking.

 

Quick Start Guide:

 This site can be used to filter for relevant heroes, provided the terms and basic mechanics of the game are understood. It lists Hero Perks and allows them to be sorted by what they affect. https://fortnitedb.com/db/Hero_Perks

 


Step 1: Understanding what a Hero Loadout Contains

  • Heroes - A hero can be one of 4 classes, and each class has a passive ability
    • Soldier - The basic gun type hero, with the goal of shooting husks better
      • Passive – Deal more damage when shooting husks
    • Ninja - Usually a melee class, with superior mobility
      • Passive – Double jump and reducing damage when hitting husks with melee
    • Constructor - Centered around empowering traps and structures, making even the walls damage husks
      • Passive – Place B.A.S.E. to enhance structures, and deal more damage when knocking down husks with melees
    • Outlander - Has the primary goal of collecting resources for crafting weapons and traps
      • Passive – Pickaxe 5 times to see nearby containers, and heavy attack pickaxe (right click) to punch through structures, collecting their materials

 

  • Hero Slots – The first slot determines the loadout’s abilities and “Commander Perk”
    • Abilities – Each hero has 3 predetermined abilities out of their class ability set
      • Soldier
        • Frag Grenade – A grenade
        • Shockwave – Area of effect slam, damaging nearby in a circle
        • War Cry – Increases attack speed and damage
        • Lefty And Righty – Equips two powerful pistols for 10 seconds
        • Goin’ Commando – Equips a minigun for 10 seconds
      • Ninja
        • Cresent Kick – A stunning kick
        • Throwing Stars – Throws 3 projectiles one after another
        • Smoke Bomb – Surrounds the player in damaging smoke
        • Kunai Storm – Launches the player, dealing damage to husks below
        • Dragon Slash – The player dashes forwards, damaging husks in the path
      • Constructor
        • Goin’ Constructor – Equips a powerful shield and hammer for 10 seconds
        • R.O.S.I.E – Places a ridable turret with limited ammo (180)
        • Bull Rush – Rushes forwards, collecting and damaging husks in the path, throwing them all forwards at the end
        • Plasma Pulse – Places a device that outputs damaging balls onto the ground that explode quickly
        • Decoy – Places a device that attracts nearby husks to attack it for 6 seconds
      • Outlander
        • T.E.D.D.Y. – Places an automated turret for 60 seconds shaped like a bear
        • Phase Rush – Dashes forwards on a low cooldown
        • Seismic Smash – Damages husks in a forwards line
        • Shock Tower – Damages nearby husks for 5 seconds
  • Perks:
    • 5 Hero Perks - Every hero has a perk that enhances the loadout
    • 1 Commander Perk – An enhanced version of a heroes’ normal perk
  • Team Perk – This slot holds a modifier that works as a stronger hero perk that requires specific heroes to be slotted. The requirements are loose, such as “3 Constructors in support team”
  • Gadgets – The last 2 slots of the loadout hold gadgets, unlocked with upgrade points in the Command tab
    • There are 8 gadgets, but only 4 have much use
      • Supply Drop – Drops suppliers such as ammo and crafting materials
      • Stationary Hover Turret – Attacks nearby husks for 30 seconds
      • Slow Field – Reduces husk speed in area by 40% for 45 seconds
      • Adrenaline Rush – Heals the player and surrounding players
      • Teleporter – The player places two movable ends of a teleporter, allowing players and projectiles to be teleported
      • Banner – Heals nearby structures and provides a respawn point for players
      • Airstrike – Drop several missiles in an area
      • Proximity Mine – Placeable mine that explodes on husks

 

  • Weapons – The weapons that are planned to be used in the mission
    • Ranged – Costs ammo, but deals damage from a safe range
    • Melee – Doesn’t cost ammo, but is more dangerous
    • Traps – Used to protect objectives from husks that aren’t killed, or often used to defend objectives entirely on their own, placed on structures

 

Step 2: Choosing what Kind of Loadout to Build

  • A loadout can focus on an ability, weapon, or even be based entirely around a specific hero perk
  • Common loadouts focus on the player’s favorite weapon, and enhances it to deal more damage or do something else that is useful such as freeze large husks
  • Other loadouts can focus on utility, such as moving faster, healing teammates, or jumping 4 times without touching the ground
  • Simply picking an ability that sounds fun or is new can make the loadout interesting and fun

 

Step 3: Building the Loadout

  • To find the heroes that affect the abilities or weapons desired, checking online would help filter the hero list into something understandable
  • This website lists all 201 different hero perks in the game. https://fortnitedb.com/db/Hero_Perks
  • The website can be set to filter for an ability, weapon type, or even just type to search for a keyword
  • Choosing the 6 hero perks for the loadout usually consists of figuring out which perks that affect the chosen focus are better than the rest

 

Step 4: How to Obtain the Heroes

  • Obtaining heroes is restricted to seasons mostly, excluding the base set that can be obtained all year round
  • Team perks are collected from obtaining mythic rarity heroes, which are above legendary
  • Most heroes can be found in x-ray llamas and event llamas, or grabbing them in the gold shop
  • The collection book also allows some heroes to be researched using legendary flux from the gold shop

 

Conclusion

Some loadouts will be created, tried out, then need total reworking. Sometimes perks do not interact as expected, or the loadout simply is not strong enough. Despite these troubles, creating a loadout that feels fun is rewarding.

Glossary

  • Abilities – A tool the player can use to do an action, such as throw a projectile or place a turret, limited by class
  • Class – A category of Hero
  • Collection Book – A game element that allows new collected heroes and weapons to be slotted for rewards in a collection
  • Commander Perk – The enhanced perk from a hero perk when said hero is slotted as commander
  • Damage – Causing harm and destruction to Husks
  • Gadgets – Similar to abilities, but not limited by class
  • Gold Shop – An area in the shop that gold collected from missions can be spent for new seasonal or weekly heroes, schematics, and other resources such as Legendary Flux
  • Heavy Attack – Right click on the mouse on melee weapons triggers a slower, more powerful attack
  • Hero Slots – The main slots of a loadout, providing hero perks to the loadout
  • Heroes – Collectable character that provide hero perks, commander perks, and the playable character
  • Husks – The main enemy of the game, resembling a horde of zombies
  • Legendary Flux – Obtained from the gold shop, it can be used to research weapons and heroes in the collection book, or upgrade an existing one
  • Llama – The main method of obtaining new weapons and heroes, randomly giving base heroes and weapons. Seasonal llamas can drop harder to find heroes and weapons
  • Loadout – The list of perks that make up the abilities and powers of the playable character
  • Onboarding - The section of a game that introduces the player to the game’s environment and mechanics
  • Passive – An ability separate from the castable abilities, providing some sort of benefit or change in how the player interacts with the game
  • Pickaxe – A tool for harvesting materials from structures
  • Research – Researching a hero means creating it from legendary flux
  • Structure – Anything built by the player or is destructible by the player
  • Team Perk – A modifier that works as a stronger hero perk that requires specific heroes to be slotted
  • Traps – A type of structure placed on player built walls, floors, or ceilings that provides an independent source of damage that can trigger automatically when husks approach it
  • Weapons – From guns to swords to traps, anything that is crafted and damages husks is a weapon
